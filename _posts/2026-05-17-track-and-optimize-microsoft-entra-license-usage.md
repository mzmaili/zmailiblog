Most teams know exactly what licenses they bought. Far fewer can show how much of that capability is actually being used. That gap costs money at renewal and, more quietly, leaves premium security controls sitting unused. **Track and Optimize Microsoft Entra License Usage is now Generally Available**, putting entitlements and feature usage in the same view.

## What the feature does

The new **License Usage** page in the Microsoft Entra admin center brings licensing and adoption together. You can see **Microsoft Entra ID P1, P2, and Suite** licensing alongside usage indicators for features such as Conditional Access and risk-based Conditional Access.

It also shows **monthly usage trends across the last six months**, so you're not looking at a single snapshot — you can see whether adoption is climbing, flat, or sliding.

## Why it matters

- **Tie spend to adoption** by viewing entitlements and feature usage in one place instead of guessing at renewal time.
- **Prove premium control adoption** for security teams that need to show Conditional Access and risk-based policies are actually in use.
- **Spot gaps and overuse** with six months of trend data for active and guest users.

## How to enable it

1. Go to **Entra admin center > Billing > Licenses**.
2. Open the **License usage** page.
3. **Review the entitlement summary**, then compare P1 and P2 hero metrics against current usage.
4. **Check the six-month pattern** for active and guest users and decide where to expand deployment or correct overuse.

## Where it fits

This is useful well beyond budget conversations. Security teams constantly need to demonstrate that the premium capabilities they're paying for — Conditional Access, risk-based policies, the broader Suite features — are deployed and exercised, not shelfware. The License Usage page gives you that evidence directly, without exporting reports or stitching together separate tools.

It also helps you plan. If you can see where premium features aren't being used, you can make a deliberate decision about where to extend protection next.

## Conclusion

This is one of those admin experiences that helps both governance and execution. Better visibility means better decisions — not just at renewal, but when you're deciding where identity protections should go next. Open the page, read the trends, and let the data drive your next move.
